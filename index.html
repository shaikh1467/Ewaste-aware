What is E-Waste?

Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices. Improper disposal of e-waste can lead to environmental pollution and health hazards due to toxic substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium.

With the rapid advancement of technology, e-waste is growing exponentially worldwide, making awareness and responsible management crucial.

Impacts of E-Waste

Toxic Chemicals

E-waste releases harmful chemicals like lead and mercury that contaminate soil and water.

Air Pollution

Burning e-waste emits toxic fumes causing respiratory problems and environmental damage.

Health Risks

Exposure to e-waste toxins can lead to neurological damage, cancer, and other serious health issues.

Environmental Damage

E-waste contributes to soil degradation, water contamination, and loss of biodiversity.

How to Reduce E-Waste

Recycle Properly

Take your old electronics to certified e-waste recycling centers to ensure safe processing and material recovery.

Reduce Consumption

Buy only what you need and choose durable electronics to minimize waste generation.

Reuse & Repair

Extend the life of your devices by repairing or donating them instead of discarding.
